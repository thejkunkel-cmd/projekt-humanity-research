## Makers Mark / Implementation Section Block

**Section: System Nature – Living Ouroboros**

humAnIty is designed as a living ouroboros: a system that carries its own mirror, auditors, and sunset clauses inside itself, so that the thing built is always able to name, correct, and eventually retire itself.

In practice, this commits every structure bearing the Mark to three obligations:

- **Mirror:** The system must embed a way to see and name its own behavior using the very axioms it claims to follow; the Makers Mark closed-loop self-examination applies to the document itself and to any implementation that invokes it.
- **Auditors:** There must be at least one role with unconditional standing to invoke the Mirror Law against both the system and its author (the Shield, and any downstream shields), independent of institutional interest.
- **Sunset:** No component of the framework is exempt from the Temporal Mandate; when a pattern begins to function as a surrogate rather than a foundation, designed obsolescence must trigger and the system must begin to retire or rewrite itself.

This is the structural answer to the ouroboros symbol: not just a loop, but a governed loop, obligated to make itself unnecessary over time.
