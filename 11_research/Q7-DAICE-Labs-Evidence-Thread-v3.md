## Q7a — Theoretical Sourcing: What DAICE Labs Actually Claims and Cites

### Core Research Framing

DAICE Labs' scientific program, visible on their research page and the "Bio-Inspired Neurosymbolic AI" documentation, draws on three theoretical pillars:

1. **Biological self-organization:** The claim that "coordinated collaboration" and "structural specialization" in living systems (cells, organs, scaffolds) are the blueprint for next-generation hybrid AI architectures. Their flagship project, "The Achiral Cell," frames a hybrid AI unit as a "living-code digital cell — an autonomous neuro-symbolic agent that manages the entire lifecycle of its own intelligence, inspired by the organizational principles of molecular biology".

2. **Thermodynamic learning:** Their *natural intelligence* page states explicitly: "For AI: formalizing architectural learning as a thermodynamic process creates systems that are not only more adaptive but more interpretable, with built-in [properties]". This thermodynamic framing is the most direct parallel to Prigogine's framework in their published material.

3. **Neurosymbolic attractor theory:** Their bio-symbolic composite AI documentation describes "emergent behavior, self-organization, and optimized resource allocation" as target properties, and their Daice Insight platform offers a dedicated module titled "Resilience in complexity: a dance with..." — signaling direct engagement with attractor/bifurcation dynamics.

### The biorXiv Preprint: "Rectifying Stress into Structural Memory"

The most technically substantive piece of published research tied to DAICE Labs (Agudelo et al., January 2026, biorXiv) connects cellular biology directly to machine learning via thermodynamic principles:

> *"Here we present a unified framework that connects cellular specialization to adaptive machine learning through principles of nonequilibrium optimization. We find that asymmetric fluctuations in key parameters act as tunable drives that, when phase-aligned, are rectified into durable functional specialization... this work identifies asymmetric-drive rectification as a fundamental mechanism of adaptive specialization, providing thermodynamically informed design rules for both bioengineering and adaptive AI."*

This preprint — funded by Daice Labs Inc., Novo Nordisk, and MIT CSAIL — is the closest DAICE has come to peer-reviewed sourcing for their thermodynamic AI claims. The framing aligns with Prigogine's dissipative structures logic (nonequilibrium optimization → local order) but does **not directly cite Prigogine's canonical works** (*Order Out of Chaos*, 1984; *From Being to Becoming*, 1980) in the abstract or publicly available excerpt.

### Prigogine Sourcing Context

Prigogine's dissipative structures framework — the idea that open systems far from equilibrium can export entropy and maintain internal order — was established in the 1967 Brussels school and awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1977. His core claim, that "order can emerge spontaneously in open systems far from equilibrium, provided they are allowed to export entropy," is the foundational concept DAICE Labs applies analogically to AI learning dynamics. The connection between fluctuation theory, bifurcation points, and AI phase transitions is a live area of research: NeurIPS 2023 featured work on "unsupervised learning for anticipating critical transitions" that treats training dynamics as a genuine second-order phase transition.
