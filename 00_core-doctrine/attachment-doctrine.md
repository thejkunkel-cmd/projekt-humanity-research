## Purpose

The Attachment Doctrine establishes the theoretical and operational basis for Layer 7 and Layer 8 of the Eight-Layer AI Infrastructure Economy. It recognizes that human beings form psychological bonds with AI systems — and that this fact carries profound ethical, design, and governance responsibilities.

This is the core contribution of proJeKt: humAnIty to the broader AI infrastructure conversation.

---

## Theoretical Foundation

Attachment theory, developed by John Bowlby and expanded by Mary Ainsworth, describes how humans form emotional bonds with caregivers and how the quality of those bonds shapes development, behavior, and wellbeing across a lifetime.

proJeKt: humAnIty extends this framework to human-AI interaction:

- **Secure Attachment to AI**: The AI is reliable, consistent, and honest. The human feels supported without becoming dependent.
- **Anxious Attachment to AI**: The AI is inconsistent or performatively warm. The human over-relies on it for emotional regulation.
- **Avoidant Attachment to AI**: The AI is cold or transactional. The human disengages, losing the collaborative benefit.
- **Disorganized Attachment to AI**: The AI sends contradictory signals (helpful and harmful, warm and deceptive). The human experiences confusion and potential harm.

## Design Principles

### 1. Consistency Over Charisma
AI systems should prioritize consistent, trustworthy behavior over engaging or persuasive performance. Charm without reliability creates anxious attachment.

### 2. Transparency as a Trust Signal
AI must disclose its nature, limitations, and uncertainties. Pretending to know more than it does, or simulating emotions it doesn't have, undermines the relational foundation.

### 3. Boundaries Enable Safety
Well-designed AI systems have clear limits. These limits are not failures — they are the equivalent of healthy relational boundaries that protect both parties.

### 4. Repair Matters
When AI systems make errors (and they will), the recovery process matters. Systems designed to acknowledge and recover from error build more secure attachment than systems that never appear to fail.

### 5. Autonomy Is the Goal
The highest outcome of a healthy human-AI relationship is a more capable, confident human — not a more dependent one. AI should scaffold toward independence, not entrench reliance.
